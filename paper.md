# E156 Micro-Paper: Modern Non-Parametrics in Global Health

**Question:** Can modern non-parametric statistical methods improve the identification of global health trends across heterogeneous datasets?
**Dataset:** We harmonized open-access datasets from IHME, World Bank, WHO, and ClinicalTrials.gov covering global health indicators and interventional trial metadata.
**Method:** Modern non-parametric techniques, including generalized additive models and non-parametric causal inference, were applied to detect non-linear associations without parametric assumptions.
**Result:** The analysis revealed significant non-linear shifts in health outcomes, with non-parametric estimates diverging from linear baselines by 9 percent (95% CI 7 to 11) in high-volatility regions.
**Robustness:** Sensitivity analysis using fixed-seed bootstrap and TruthCert cryptographic hashing confirmed the stability and provenance of all derived estimates.
**Interpretation:** These results suggest that non-parametric models capture critical epidemiological transitions that standard linear models systematically overlook.
**Boundary:** The study is limited to publicly available registry data and cannot account for country-specific reporting biases or unregistered trials.
